03. Quantum Foundation

From Bits to
Qubits

0/1

Classical Bit

Deterministic State

Either 0 OR 1. Like a light switch—on or off.

|ψ⟩

Quantum Qubit

Superposition State

Both 0 AND 1 simultaneously. Like a spinning coin—heads and tails at once.

⚛️

Superposition

n qubits = 2ⁿ states at once

🔗

Entanglement

Instant correlation between qubits

⚡

Quantum Parallelism

Operate on all states simultaneously

50 Qubits

Can represent 2⁵⁰ ≈ 1 quadrillion states simultaneously

Classical computers would need 1 PB of RAM just to store these states!

04. Core Concept

Quantum Principles
Transform Search

Classical

Sequential Search

1️⃣ Evaluate solution #1
2️⃣ Evaluate solution #2
... Continue sequentially
🐢 Time: O(2ⁿ) — Exponential
Quantum

Parallel Search

🌊 Initialize superposition (all states)
🎯 Apply quantum oracle (mark solutions)
📈 Amplify good solutions
⚡ Time: O(√2ⁿ) — Quadratic speedup!
05. Key Algorithms

Three Pillars of
Quantum Optimization

❄️

Quantum Annealing

Exploit quantum tunneling to escape local minima and find global optimum

Start in quantum superposition
Gradually reduce fluctuations
Settle to ground state
Best for: Ising/QUBO problems
Hardware: D-Wave
🎛️

QAOA

Hybrid quantum-classical algorithm for NISQ devices

Prepare quantum state
Alternate Hamiltonians
Classical optimization loop
Best for: MaxCut, MaxSAT
Type: Hybrid NISQ
🔍

Grover's Algorithm

Quadratic speedup for unstructured search problems

Initialize uniform superposition
Oracle marks targets
Amplitude amplification
Best for: Database search
Speedup: √N (Optimal)

08. Challenges

Roadblocks to
Quantum Supremacy

📡

Noise & Decoherence

Qubits lose quantum properties due to environmental interference

Gate errors: 0.1-1% per operation
Coherence: Microseconds
🔧

Hardware Limits

Limited qubit count and connectivity constraints

Current: 100-1000 qubits
Needed: Millions (logical)
📈

Scalability

Mapping real problems to quantum hardware

Connectivity: Limited
Encoding: Overhead heavy

Mitigation Strategies

🛡️

Error Correction

Surface codes, logical qubits

🔗

Hybrid Algorithms

Quantum + classical

❄️

Better Hardware

Cryogenic systems

⚙️

Compilers

Optimized circuit mapping

Reality Check: Current quantum computers are in the NISQ (Noisy Intermediate-Scale Quantum) era. We're still 5-10 years away from fault-tolerant quantum computers that can solve commercially relevant optimization problems at scale.
